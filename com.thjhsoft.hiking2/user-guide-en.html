6. FAQ

Q1: Why are step counts inaccurate?

A: Step counting relies on your phone's motion sensor, and accuracy is affected by:

  • Phone placement (pocket is more accurate than handheld)
  • Walking style (normal walking is more accurate than shuffling)
  • Sensor quality (different phones have different sensor accuracy)
ğŸ’¡ Suggestion: Keep your phone in your pocket or backpack and maintain a steady walking rhythm.

Q2: Does the app drain battery?

A: Hiking Step Counter is optimized with very low battery consumption:

  • Uses system step counter sensor, much more efficient than GPS tracking
  • Default "UI Level" sensitivity already balanced
  • Background service is lightweight with small memory footprint
ğŸ’¡ Battery Saving Tip: For maximum battery efficiency, switch to "Power Saving" mode. Accuracy slightly decreases but remains acceptable.

Q3: Why does the notification bar always show?

A: This is a foreground service notification, required by Android:

  • Foreground services must display notification to keep users informed
  • This prevents the system from killing the step counting service
  • You can disable the badge (dot) in notification settings

Q4: Will data be lost after uninstalling?

A: Yes. Since the app doesn't upload any data to the cloud, local data will be cleared upon uninstall.

âš ï¸ Note: The app currently doesn't provide export functionality. To keep your data, don't uninstall the app.

Q5: Which Android versions are supported?

A: Android 8.0 (API 26) and above.

Q6: Why do steps sometimes suddenly decrease?

A: Possible reasons:

  • Phone restarted, sensor counter was reset
  • System killed the step counting service (recommended to add app to battery whitelist)
ğŸ’¡ Solution: Add "Hiking Step Counter" to battery whitelist or auto-start whitelist in phone settings.
