1. App Introduction

Hiking Step Counter is a simple, efficient, privacy-focused step counting app. It uses your phone's built-in motion sensor to automatically record your steps, helping you develop healthy exercise habits.

✨ Core Features

  • 🔒 100% Local Storage - Data never uploaded, privacy protected
  • 🎨 Beautiful Design - Material Design 3 style with dark mode support
  • 📊 Detailed Statistics - View data by hour/day/week/month
  • ⚡ Efficient & Battery-Saving - Adjustable sensor sensitivity to balance accuracy and battery life
  • 💪 Daily Motivation - Encouraging messages to help you stay active
  • 🆓 Completely Free - No ads, no in-app purchases

2. Core Features

2.1 Real-time Step Counting

The app uses a foreground service to continuously monitor your phone's step counter sensor (TYPE_STEP_COUNTER), automatically recording your steps.

  • 📈 Real-time updates: Step changes displayed immediately on main screen and notification
  • 🔄 Auto-save: Data saved automatically every minute, no worries about app crashes
  • 🌙 Background operation: Continues counting even when screen is locked

2.2 Progress Visualization

The main screen features a large circular progress indicator showing today's completion:

  • 🎯 Displays today's total steps and completion percentage
  • ✨ Cool glowing animation and rotation effects
  • 🎨 Colors change dynamically based on completion

2.3 Data Statistics

Click "View Data" button to see detailed statistics:

  • Today: Hourly display showing daily step distribution
  • This Week: Daily display comparing each day's activity
  • This Month: Daily display showing monthly trends

Each view provides: total steps, average, and maximum values.

2.4 Daily Motivation

Automatically sends a daily motivational message to encourage you to stay active (can be disabled in settings).

3. Quick Start

Step 1: Accept Privacy Policy

  1. When you first open the app, a privacy policy dialog appears
  2. Read carefully, then click "Agree and Continue"
  3. To view the complete policy, click "View Full Privacy Policy"
⚠️ Note: If you click "Decline", the app will close automatically. We respect your choice!

Step 2: Grant Permissions

After accepting the policy, the system will request the following permissions:

  • Activity Recognition: Required, used to access step counter sensor
  • Notification: Recommended, used to display step counting status
💡 Tip: If you accidentally deny permissions, you can manually enable them in System Settings → Apps → Hiking Step Counter → Permissions.

Step 3: Start Step Counting

  1. Find the "Tracking Switch" card on the main screen
  2. Click the switch button on the right
  3. See "✅ Tracking" message, indicating step counting service has started
  4. Notification bar will display step count notification showing today's steps in real-time

Step 4: Start Walking

Now you can put your phone in your pocket and walk normally. The app will automatically record your steps!

4. Settings Guide

Click the "⚙️ Settings" button in the top right of the main screen to enter settings.

4.1 Daily Goal

  • Default goal: 10,000 steps
  • Customizable: Enter any positive integer
  • Takes effect immediately: Updates main screen progress after modification

4.2 Sensor Sensitivity

Choose appropriate sensitivity based on your needs:

  • 🚀 Fastest: Highest accuracy, most battery drain (~5ms sampling interval)
  • 🎮 Game: High accuracy, moderate battery drain (~20ms)
  • 📱 UI Level: Recommended! Balances accuracy and battery (~60ms)
  • 🔋 Power Saving: Standard accuracy, most battery efficient (~200ms)
⚠️ Important: After changing sensitivity, you need to turn off and restart the step counting service for it to take effect!

4.3 Motivational Reminders

  • When enabled, you'll receive one motivational notification per day
  • Notification time: About 2 seconds after app starts
  • Can be disabled at any time

6. FAQ

Q1: Why are step counts inaccurate?

A: Step counting relies on your phone's motion sensor, and accuracy is affected by:

  • Phone placement (pocket is more accurate than handheld)
  • Walking style (normal walking is more accurate than shuffling)
  • Sensor quality (different phones have different sensor accuracy)
💡 Suggestion: Keep your phone in your pocket or backpack and maintain a steady walking rhythm.

Q2: Does the app drain battery?

A: Hiking Step Counter is optimized with very low battery consumption:

  • Uses system step counter sensor, much more efficient than GPS tracking
  • Default "UI Level" sensitivity already balanced
  • Background service is lightweight with small memory footprint
💡 Battery Saving Tip: For maximum battery efficiency, switch to "Power Saving" mode. Accuracy slightly decreases but remains acceptable.

Q3: Why does the notification bar always show?

A: This is a foreground service notification, required by Android:

  • Foreground services must display notification to keep users informed
  • This prevents the system from killing the step counting service
  • You can disable the badge (dot) in notification settings

Q4: Will data be lost after uninstalling?

A: Yes. Since the app doesn't upload any data to the cloud, local data will be cleared upon uninstall.

⚠️ Note: The app currently doesn't provide export functionality. To keep your data, don't uninstall the app.

Q5: Which Android versions are supported?

A: Android 8.0 (API 26) and above.

Q6: Why do steps sometimes suddenly decrease?

A: Possible reasons:

  • Phone restarted, sensor counter was reset
  • System killed the step counting service (recommended to add app to battery whitelist)
💡 Solution: Add "Hiking Step Counter" to battery whitelist or auto-start whitelist in phone settings.

7. Usage Tips

Tip 1: Whitelist Settings

To ensure stable operation of step counting service, add the app to these whitelists:

  • Battery Whitelist: Prevent system hibernation
  • Auto-start Whitelist: Auto-start after boot (if needed)
  • Lock Screen Cleanup Whitelist: Continue running after screen lock

Specific settings path varies by phone brand, usually found in Settings → Battery → Power Saving Mode.

Tip 2: Set Reasonable Goals

  • Beginners: 5,000 - 8,000 steps
  • General population: 8,000 - 10,000 steps (WHO recommended)
  • Fitness enthusiasts: 12,000 - 15,000 steps
  • Professional level: 15,000+ steps

Tip 3: View Trends

Regularly check "This Week" and "This Month" data to understand exercise patterns:

  • Identify days with most activity and analyze reasons
  • Discover periods of insufficient exercise and make improvement plans
  • Set weekly/monthly challenge goals

Tip 4: Disable Unnecessary Notifications

If you don't need motivational reminders:

  1. Go to settings page
  2. Find "Motivational Reminders" switch
  3. Turn it off
