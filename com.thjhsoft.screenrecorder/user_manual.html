📹 Screen Recorder

User Manual  ·  Version 1.0  ·  March 10, 2026

🚀 Getting Started

Screen Recorder lets you capture everything on your screen with audio, camera overlay, and flexible settings — all from one app.

On first launch, grant the required permissions:

  • Microphone – to record your voice.
  • Camera – to show a camera preview overlay during recording.
  • Display over other apps – for the floating control button.
  • Notifications – (Android 13+) to show the recording status notification.

🎬 Starting a Recording

  1. Open the app and configure your settings on the Record tab.
  2. Tap the large red Record button.
  3. Accept the media-projection permission prompt.
  4. A 3-second countdown starts, then recording begins.
💡 A floating stop button appears on screen so you can stop recording at any time.

🎙️ Audio Settings

  • Microphone – toggle to include / exclude your voice.
  • System Audio – toggle to capture internal device audio (music, alerts, etc.).
  • Audio Mix Ratio – slider to balance microphone vs system audio level.

📷 Camera Settings

Enable a live camera preview that floats on screen while recording.

  • Front Camera – shows a preview from the selfie camera.
  • Rear Camera – shows a preview from the main camera.

Only one camera can be active at a time. On the overlay you can:

  • Drag the preview window to reposition it.
  • Drag the ↙ handle (bottom-right) to resize it (keeping 16:9 ratio).
  • Tap ✕ (top-right) to close the camera preview.

🖥️ Resolution

  • 1080p – Full HD, larger file size.
  • 720p – HD, smaller file size, better for older devices.

📂 Recordings Library

Switch to the Videos tab to browse all your recordings.

  • Tap a video to play it.
  • Long-press to enter multi-selection mode, then share or delete.
  • Recordings are saved to your device's Movies / ScreenRecorder folder.

⚙️ Settings

  • Dark Mode – choose Follow System, Light, or Dark theme.
